Snake Plant

Water when top inch of soil feels dry, water once a week, allow water to drain. Never allow the soil to become saturated.

Tolerates low, medium and bright light. Can grow in any room in your home, or office.

Toxins absorbed by the plant

Trichloroethylene
Formaldehyde
Benzene
Xylene

Rubber Plant

Water the top inch of soil of the Rubber Plant once the soil feels dry. Never allow the soil to become saturated.

Rubber plant happily grows in bright light. Light helps their leaves retain their burgundy color.

Toxins absorbed by the plant

Trichloroethylene
Formaldehyde
Benzene
Xylene

Spider Plant

Let fully dry out between watering

spider plants thrive best in bright light or partial direct sun. Be careful not to give them too much sun or leaves will begin to burn, turning a brown color

Toxins absorbed by the plant

Formaldehyde
Xylene
Bezene

Aloe Vera

Water your Aloe vera every 2-4 weeks. Don't over water. If Aloe begins to smell, then it’s rotting. Dark, transparent spots or crimping at the base of the leaves are also signs of too much water.

Needs bright light - do best in south- or west-facing windows.

Toxins absorbed by the plant

Formaldehyde
Benzene
carbon monoxide

English Ivy

When watering your ivy, always check the soil before adding water. Ivies prefer to be kept slightly on the dry side, so let the soil dry out some (dry to the touch on top) before you water your ivy plant again.

A fan of bright light to partial shade, ivy are great creeping or climbing plants and are one of the best house plants for cleaning and purifying air

Toxins absorbed by the plant

Trichloroethylene
Formaldehyde
Benzene
Xylene

Bamboo Palm

Water the palm using room temperature filtered water when the soil surface feels dry. Water the plant until the soil is evenly moist. Don’t over water the palm plant or leave it sitting in water.

Bamboo palm will grow in low light conditions, although they will grow taller with more light.

Toxins absorbed by the plant

Formaldehyde
Xylene
